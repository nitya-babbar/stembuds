WHERE

Tier-1 elementary schools in the Nashua School District, where at least 80% of the students are on a meal plan: Amherst Street, Birch Hill, Doctor Crisp, Fairgrounds, Ledge Street, Mount Pleasant, and Sunset Heights.


CURRENT PROBLEM

Students from lower income families don’t have the same opportunities that other students benefit from and lack proper academic support outside of the classroom. As a result, they fall behind and ultimately give up on school.


OUR SOLUTION

High school volunteers work with students for two hours, once a week. The ratio of students to volunteers is about three to one. Students recieve help on homework, and then do a fun STEM activity that develops their STEM interest.

COVID-19 CHANGES

Due to COVID-19, the Nashua schools are currently fully remote. However, we did not want this to prevent students from extending their STEM experiences and stop them from bonding with our high school volunteers. So, we are currently holding Zoom sessions for one hour a week for all students that are a part of STEMBuds. During this time, STEMBuds students are using Scratch, and developing projects of their own. Scratch is a great introduction to coding. We believe that learning to code instills great analytical thinking and problem solving skills in students. Students are getting 1 on 1 attention to connect with a high school mentor and design their own Scratch projects. All STEMBuds volunteers are well versed in Scratch to help students develop a project of their imaginations.

The Very First STEMBuds Session

Oct. 17
2016

The weekly sessions at the five tier-1 Nashua elementary schools began for the 2016-2017 school year!

Junior First Lego League

Apr. 2 - Jun. 4
2018

STEMBuds volunteers held ten 75-minute sessions, where we led six Junior First Lego League teams each consisting of four second and third graders. The program was a great success, and we were able to influence kids to enjoy STEM fields at a very young age!

First Lego League

Sep. 6 - Dec. 13
2019

The STEMBuds volunteers helped Bicentennial Elementary School and Main Dunstable Elementary School prepare for their First Lego League competition. 15 volunteers went to weekly meetings held at both schools for 2 hours every week to help with robot design, programming, and projects.

Trimester 1 Begins (With 4 More Schools)

Oct. 1
2019

The first trimester of the new school year began. A curriculum is set in stone, with a rotation of engineering, science, and then math every three weeks. This year we are working with 9 schools, 4 more than last year!
